Sambusas

When I was working for corporate America, I brought some Sambusas to a potluck and one of my senior co workers came up to me after. "Kassim, I have a business pitch for you. Bring the Sambusas more often but sell it to us"

Business was booming and I was considering getting a food truck! Sambusas are simply amazing. Anyone who tries them always wants more. You can fill the insides with whatever you desire: chicken, beef, or fish. You can make it 100% vegetarian or add a vegan twist to it. So not only does it taste great, but it is versatile catering to anyone. This is a dish you can never go wrong with.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • ⅓ cup warm water
  • 3 tablespoons canola oil, plus more for frying
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 large baking potato, coarsely chopped
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, stemmed, seeded, and finely diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Xawaash Spice Mix (recipe here)
  • ½ pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, finely chopped

Steps

  1. Put the flour, water, 1 tablespoon of the oil, and a large pinch of salt in a large bowl and stir with a wooden spoon until a dough forms.
  2. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured work surface and knead until it is smooth, elastic, soft, and doesn’t stick to your fingers, about 5 minutes.
  3. Return the dough to the bowl you mixed it in, lightly dust the dough with flour, and cover with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap.
  4. Set it aside to rest until soft and slightly risen, about 30 minutes.
  5. While the dough rests, place the potato and a large pinch of salt in a small saucepan and cover with cold water.
  6. Set the saucepan over high heat and bring the water to a boil.
  7. Lower the heat to maintain a simmer and cook until the potato is tender, about 15 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  8. Meanwhile, warm the remaining 2 tablespoons oil in a large skillet set over medium heat.
  9. Add the onion, bell pepper, garlic, and Xawaash.
  10. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion begins to soften, about 5 minutes.
  11. Add the chicken and sprinkle with a large pinch of salt.
  12. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through, about 10 minutes.
  13. Turn off the heat and stir in the potato. The whole mixture should be like a rough mash.
  14. Let the filling cool to room temperature, then season it to taste with salt.
  15. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured work surface and divide it in half.
  16. Use a floured rolling pin to roll each piece into a thin 12-inch square (or as close to a square as you can get).
  17. If the dough resists as you’re rolling it, just let it rest for a few minutes before proceeding.
  18. Cut each square into four even strips. Divide the filling among the strips, placing it in a mound at one end of each strip, about 1 inch from the edge.
  19. Working with one strip at a time, fold the outer inch of the dough up and over the filling to form a triangle.
  20. Take the outer end and fold it down to add another layer of dough onto the triangle.
  21. Continue folding up and down in a zigzag to continue encasing the filling in more layers of dough until you reach the end of the strip.
  22. Repeat the entire process with the remaining dough and filling. You will have eight triangles by the time you’re done.
  23. Line a plate or tray with paper towels and set aside.
  24. Heat 1 inch of oil in a Dutch oven or other heavy pot set over medium-high heat until it reaches 375°F on an instant- read thermometer or until the edge of a samosa dipped into the oil sizzles on contact.
  25. Fry the samosas, in batches if possible, depending on the size of your pot, until they are browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per samosa.
  26. Transfer the browned samosas to the prepared plate or tray to drain.
  27. Serve and enjoy!

Note: Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a few days and rewarmed on a baking sheet, uncovered, in a 350ºF oven for about 10 minutes before serving.
